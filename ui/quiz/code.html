Q1 · Multiple Choice Medium

In a quantum system, what does a state in a superposition physically represent before measurement?

lightbulb Explanation

expand_less

Superposition is a fundamental principle of quantum mechanics. It states that, much like waves in classical physics, any two (or more) quantum states can be added together ("superposed") and the result will be another valid quantum state. It does not mean the system is rapidly switching, but rather exists in all these states mathematically until observation collapses the wave function.
