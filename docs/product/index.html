Product boundary

Who this is for—and what success means

Primary audience

Recruiters, interviewers, and engineers evaluating AI full-stack design, backend depth, data contracts, reliability, and delivery discipline.

Demo user

A researcher, learner, or analyst who needs to review a large bounded source collection and preserve the evidence behind every conclusion.

Portfolio promise

Every important claim about the system should be inspectable through code, contracts, automated tests, evaluation results, runtime traces, or deployment evidence.

First meaningful success

A reader can understand the architecture quickly, run a grounded workflow, inspect its evidence and trace, and discuss at least one measured engineering trade-off.

Commercial state: this is intentionally not a commercial SaaS. There is no target buyer, price, subscription, payment flow, sales funnel, or revenue objective. Demo usage validates engineering behavior rather than market conversion.

Primary scenario

Multi-source Evidence Review Pipeline

The target workflow is deliberately more demanding than document chat. It needs long-running work, parallel evidence extraction, durable decisions, incremental recomputation, and quality measurement.

1 · DefineCreate an owner-scoped project with a research goal, constraints, and tens to hundreds of documents or web sources.
2 · IngestValidate, upload, checksum, parse, deduplicate, version, chunk, embed, and index sources through retryable background jobs.
3 · PlanDecompose the research goal into bounded sub-questions and persist the LangGraph state required to pause, resume, or replay.
4 · ExtractRetrieve evidence in parallel, extract structured claims, preserve exact quotations and locations, and measure coverage.
5 · ReconcileClassify claim relationships as supporting, contradicting, duplicating, or merely mentioning; surface unresolved gaps.
6 · ReviewPause low-confidence conclusions, untrusted web evidence, and prompt-injection signals for an approve, edit, or reject decision.
7 · SynthesizeGenerate an evidence-backed report, then refresh only impacted claims and sections when sources change.
8 · ProveRun versioned evaluation experiments and inspect traces, latency, token use, cost, errors, retries, and recovery.
Failure and recovery contract: a provider timeout retries within policy; a poisoned ingestion item becomes a visible failed job; an uncertain conclusion pauses for review; a process restart resumes from a durable checkpoint; a cross-store deletion is reconciled until every target reaches a terminal state.
